Detailed Diagnostic Report (DDR)

1.  Property Issue Summary The inspection identified multiple dampness
    and leakage issues in Flat No. 103, mainly at the skirting level of
    rooms and walls, along with bathroom tile joint gaps and plumbing
    issues.

Key problems observed include: - Dampness at the skirting level in Hall,
Bedroom, Master Bedroom, and Kitchen. - Wall dampness and efflorescence
in the Master Bedroom. - Leakage observed in the parking ceiling below
the flat. - Mild dampness in the common bathroom ceiling. - Tile joint
gaps and plumbing-related issues in bathrooms. - Cracks observed on the
external wall near the master bedroom.

Thermal imaging analysis indicates temperature variations between damp
and dry areas, with colder spots typically between 20.1°C and 23.4°C,
suggesting possible moisture presence inside the walls or floor area.

Overall inspection checklist rating shows moderate structural condition
with leakage issues present.

2.  Area-wise Observations

Hall - Dampness observed at the skirting level of the hall wall. -
Thermal images show lower temperature zones near the base of the wall,
indicating moisture accumulation.

Common Bedroom - Dampness detected at the skirting level of the bedroom
wall. - Thermal images indicate cooler regions near the floor-wall
junction, suggesting moisture seepage from the floor or adjacent areas.

Master Bedroom - Dampness observed at the skirting level. - Wall
dampness and efflorescence noted on the master bedroom wall surface. -
Thermal images show temperature differences indicating moisture
penetration inside the wall.

Kitchen - Dampness observed at the skirting level of the kitchen wall. -
Thermal imaging also shows cool zones along the wall base, indicating
moisture accumulation.

Bathrooms (Common Bathroom and Master Bedroom Bathroom) - Gaps observed
between tile joints. - Gaps around Nahani trap joints and possible
plumbing-related leakage. - Some tiles show hollowness, indicating weak
bonding. - Water leakage observed around plumbing fixtures.

External Wall - Cracks observed on the external wall near the master
bedroom. - Possible pathways for water infiltration from outside.

Parking Area - Leakage observed in the parking ceiling below Flat
No.103, indicating water penetration through the slab.

Common Bathroom Ceiling - Mild dampness observed on the ceiling,
possibly due to leakage from an upper floor or plumbing line.

3.  Probable Root Cause Based on inspection observations and thermal
    analysis, the likely causes include:

4.  Bathroom tile joint gaps allowing water penetration.

5.  Possible concealed plumbing leakage.

6.  Damaged waterproofing below bathroom flooring.

7.  External wall cracks allowing rainwater infiltration.

8.  Water migration through slab affecting parking ceiling.

9.  Poor tile adhesion causing water accumulation beneath tiles.

10. Severity Assessment (with reasoning) Severity Level: Moderate

Reasoning: - Multiple rooms are affected by dampness and seepage. -
Leakage has reached the parking ceiling, indicating deeper moisture
movement. - Thermal imaging confirms moisture accumulation inside walls
and skirting areas. - Structural RCC components show moderate crack
conditions but no major corrosion or spalling.

5.  Recommended Actions

Bathroom Repairs - Remove and re-grout tile joints in the common and
master bathrooms. - Repair gaps around the Nahani trap and drain
outlets. - Inspect and repair concealed plumbing lines if leakage is
detected.

Waterproofing - Apply waterproofing treatment beneath bathroom
flooring. - Seal all tile joints and plumbing penetrations.

External Wall Repairs - Seal and repair cracks in the external wall near
the master bedroom. - Apply external waterproof coating or crack
sealant.

Wall Treatment - Remove damaged plaster in damp areas. - Allow walls to
dry and apply anti-damp waterproof plaster or coating.

Parking Ceiling - Inspect the slab area above the parking ceiling. -
Repair leakage source and re-plaster the affected ceiling.

Preventive Measures - Regular inspection of plumbing fixtures and
bathroom waterproofing. - Maintain proper sealing of tile joints.

6.  Additional Notes Thermal inspection showed consistent temperature
    differences between dry and damp areas, confirming the presence of
    moisture inside the walls and floor junctions. Most dampness appears
    concentrated at the skirting level, suggesting seepage from floor or
    plumbing sources rather than roof leakage. Bathrooms appear to be
    the primary moisture source affecting adjacent rooms.
